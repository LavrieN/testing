List of Mechanical Parts

Gear

Description: A circular piece with teeth that mesh with another gear to transfer rotation. Gears of different ratios can be connected to increase or decrease speed, which conversely decreases or increases torque.

Chain Drive

Description: A chain drive consists of multiple rigid metal links joined together to form a chain. These links fit onto sprockets to transfer rotation efficiently over longer distances.

Belt Drive

Description: A belt drive is similar to a chain drive, but uses a continuous flexible loop of material. Belts can be positive-driven by teeth (synchronous/timing belts) or friction-driven (flat or V-belts). Friction-driven belts can act as a mechanical safety slip if torque limits are exceeded.
